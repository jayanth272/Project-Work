Evolution of Anime:

Although japanese animation can be seen as early as 1917, it really became mainstream in the 60’s, with shows like Astro Boy and Speed Racer. Most of the anime produced up till the 90’s (and even after) were targeted towards specific genders and age groups. Eventually, specific genre emerged within these, like the 'magical girl' genre Sailor Moon brought about. Throughout the 70’s and 80’s, as anime grew more widespread, it became more diverse and complex in its target audiences and genre. Over this period, art styles changed, due to advanced technology and technique, as well as organic evolution as public tastes changed. Colors became more vivid, frames more smooth, and the style more realistic and well drawn. In the 90’s anime in the west exploded. Dragon Ball, Pokemon, Neon Genesis, Gundam, Cowboy Bebop (just to name a few) became extremely popular. With the increased fanbase and demand came better quality and more diversity. More mature and sophisticated anime became more popular.
